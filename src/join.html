How to Join a Club

Getting involved is easy. Follow our simple steps to register, meet your team, and start making an impact.

Registration Steps

Complete these five steps to officially become a member of any UCP student club.

1

Explore Available Clubs

Browse the Club Categories page to discover all active clubs and societies at UCP. Read about each club's focus area and current membership.

2

Submit Your Interest Form

Visit the Student Affairs Office (SAO) or the club's dedicated desk during orientation week to fill out the membership interest form. You can also fill it out online through the UCP student portal.

3

Attend an Orientation Session

Each club holds a brief orientation at the start of each semester. Attend to meet the club president, understand the code of conduct, and learn about ongoing and upcoming activities.

4

Pay the Membership Fee

A nominal semester membership fee of PKR 200–500 (varies by club) is required. This is paid at the Finance Office and a receipt is provided. Some clubs offer fee waivers — ask the club coordinator.

5

Receive Your Member ID & Start Participating

Once confirmed, you will receive a digital member ID via email. You are now officially a club member. Attend weekly meetings, participate in events, and contribute to club projects!

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I join more than one club?
Yes! There is no limit to the number of clubs you can join. However, we recommend starting with one or two to balance your academic commitments.
Is membership open to all semesters?
Yes, clubs are open to students from all semesters — from 1st semester freshers to final-year students. Some leadership positions may require you to be at least in your 3rd semester.
What if I miss the orientation session?
Contact the club coordinator directly via email or visit the Student Affairs Office. Late registrations are usually accepted within the first two weeks of the semester.
Can I start my own club?
Absolutely! Submit a Club Formation Proposal to the Student Affairs Office. You will need at least 15 founding members, a faculty advisor, and an approved charter to proceed.
